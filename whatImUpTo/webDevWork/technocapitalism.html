Technocapitalism: A Redesign

View Prototype View Webpage

Overview

This was a group assignment for my Intro to Design studies class, which focused on how we can design ethically, based on social need and accessibility. With the entire group being interested in Tech, we decided to research how modern tech shapes the society around us and build a contemporary website based on said research. Whilst browsing, we came across the term Technocapitalism, coined by a popular author Luis Suarez-Villa. My role was to co-design in Figma and do all the development. My group members took care of research.


Design

Whilst the information on Prof. Suarez Villa's site was still exceedingly relevant, the design was a bit outdated. So we decided to reach out to him and propose a redesign alongside being able to mix our own research with his own ideas. With this being for a final project I wanted to take some inspiration from the style of PowerPoint/Keynote presentations, hence the bold titles.
I didn't want to completely stray from the original page so I decided to keep the black, yellow and white color palette but made it occupy more space. The contrast on the original site is a bit jarring, so I kept the theme of strong contrast but decided to use the difference in colors more tastefully.
I enjoyed the idea of having an index page that appeared to have no information but viewers could explore via the arrows and discover. There's an intangible sense of reward that users feel when they hover, see an animation then click the arrow to reveal more info. View Repository


Target Audience

A lot of the information covered here is intentionally surface level. We live in an era where so many terms are being thrown around and people that aren't overtly asking questions may have a hard time understanding said language. Whilst there is a more in depth research paper attached to the project. I wanted to keep the wording simple because if for instance you were to go on Google Chrome and search "What is a BlockChain?" you're often given a definition that leaves you with further questions.


Reflections

As all good User Interfaces should be, this is a work in progress. I'd like to acknowledge that at the time of reading this the site may not yet be responsive. It being in the midst of finals it was developed only for Desktop view. However, as I find the time over summer break, I intend to make it responsive and potentially even add to the research element because someone looking for more in depth analysis may find it a bit sparse.
